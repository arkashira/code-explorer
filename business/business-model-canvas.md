# Business Model Canvas for Code Explorer

## Customer Segments
- Software development teams in tech companies (startups to enterprises).
- Freelance developers and consultants working on diverse projects.
- Educational institutions teaching programming and software engineering.
- Open-source contributors needing to navigate complex codebases.

## Value Propositions
- AI-driven personalized explanations that enhance understanding of complex codebases.
- Time-saving navigation tools that reduce onboarding time for new developers.
- Improved code comprehension leading to higher productivity and fewer errors.
- Integration with popular IDEs and version control systems for seamless use.

## Channels
- Direct sales through the official website and GitHub repository.
- Partnerships with educational institutions for student licenses.
- Online marketplaces (e.g., GitHub Marketplace, AWS Marketplace).
- Developer community forums and tech conferences for demonstrations and workshops.

## Customer Relationships
- Dedicated customer support via chat and email for troubleshooting.
- Regular updates and feature enhancements based on user feedback.
- Community forums for users to share tips, tricks, and best practices.
- Webinars and tutorials to help users maximize the tool's potential.

## Revenue Streams
- Subscription model: Monthly fee of $19 (approx. 670 THB) per user.
- Enterprise licenses for larger teams starting at $499 (approx. 17,500 THB) per month.
- Educational discounts for institutions at $9 (approx. 315 THB) per student per month.
- One-time fees for premium features or additional integrations.

## Key Resources
- AI algorithms and models for code analysis and explanation generation.
- Development team for ongoing product enhancements and support.
- Marketing team for outreach and customer acquisition strategies.
- Infrastructure for hosting and maintaining the application.

## Key Activities
- Continuous development and improvement of AI models for better accuracy.
- User experience research to refine navigation and explanation features.
- Marketing campaigns to raise awareness and attract new users.
- Customer support and community engagement to foster loyalty.

## Key Partners
- Educational institutions for collaborative programs and student licenses.
- Software development tool providers for integration partnerships.
- Tech conferences and meetups for promotional opportunities.
- Influential developers and tech bloggers for endorsements and reviews.

## Cost Structure
- Development costs: Salaries for engineers and data scientists.
- Marketing expenses: Online advertising, event sponsorships, and promotional materials.
- Infrastructure costs: Cloud hosting and maintenance fees.
- Customer support costs: Salaries for support staff and tools for communication.
